Description: <p>▪Flute<br>Performer: Seokhee Lee<br>Acoustic Technique Exploration Based on Bärenreiter's 'The Techniques of Flute Playing,' we concentrated on exploring multiphonics. We attempted various fingering combinations that produce two or more simultaneous pitches, focusing on securing complex acoustic sources. We also placed weight on techniques utilizing breath and air sounds. We explored diverse textures by employing 'jet whistles' (strong air sounds), 'air sounds' (emphasizing breath over distinct pitch), and combining these with flutter tonguing or bisbigliando. Furthermore, we recorded percussive techniques such as 'tongue rams,' 'sputato' (producing plosive sounds with the lips), and 'key clicks.' We also collected a broad range of non-listed samples, including 'singing' (vocalizing while playing) and 'harmonic clusters.'</p><p>▪Percussion<br>Performer: Seokhee Lee<br>For the vibraphone, we actively utilized 'prepared' techniques beyond standard playing methods to secure a variety of textures. We placed various objects—such as castanets, coins, paper, plastic cups, aluminum foil, and juju shakers—on the bars to alter their inherent timbre. We also expanded the acoustic possibilities through non-traditional techniques, including bowing with a cello bow, scraping with metal sticks, harmonics using soft mallets, muting techniques, and clusters.<br>The recording for drums (snare and toms) focused on friction sounds using jazz brushes or superball mallets, in addition to standard playing. For cymbals and hi-hats, we dedicated significant time to creating friction sounds, such as bowing with a double bass bow or scraping the dome and edge with sticks or brushes. For the hi-hat, we systematically explored sounds resulting from different open/closed states, including foot splashes, half-open positions, and irregular open patterns.</p>
