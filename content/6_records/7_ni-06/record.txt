Description: <p>▪Flute<br>Performer: Hyewon Kim<br>Effects &amp; Improvisation With flutist Hyewon Kim, we explored extending the flute’s sonic possibilities using various effects pedals such as delay, distortion, and loops. In particular, we recorded improvisations where the performer listened to pre-recorded files and reacted to them in real-time, capturing the interaction between the effects and the performer.</p><p>▪Saxophone<br>Performer: Iun Son<br>Together with saxophonist Iun Son, we focused on exploring contemporary techniques for the alto saxophone, based on Bärenreiter's 'The Techniques of Saxophone Playing.' We paid particular attention to the combination of multiple techniques. We attempted various combinations of air sounds, bisbigliando, key clicks, flutter tonguing, and singing. By simultaneously applying three or more techniques, we aimed to realize complex and unique sounds. 'Slap tonguing' was also approached in detail, categorized by whether the keys were open or closed, producing dry ('secco') textures, or connecting to a pitch after the slap. Additionally, we attempted varied permutations, including playing with teeth on the reed, whistle tones, sub-tones, various glissando versions, and expressions in the extremely high register (altissimo) or at extremely quiet dynamics (ppp).</p><p>▪Bass Guitar<br>Performer: Hyuk Son<br>With bassist Hyuk Son, we placed significant weight on 'prepared' techniques, such as inserting plastic cards between the strings or rubbing objects on the strings. We also emphasized the instrument's percussive characteristics. We recorded aggressive sounds like combinations of slapping and plucking, as well as 'Bartók pizzicato' (snapping the string against the fingerboard). In addition to standard tapping, we attempted left-hand tapping and harmonics tapping using irregular patterns to experiment with unique rhythms and textures.</p>
