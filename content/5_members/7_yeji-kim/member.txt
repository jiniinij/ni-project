Introduction: <p>Yeji Kim is a haegeum performer, composer, and researcher who builds a unique musical world bridging tradition and modernity. She holds degrees from Dankook University and Seoul National University (MA) and served as a member of the Cheongju Gugak Orchestra (2019–2024). As a member of the electroacoustic trio ‘Samkim,’ her innovative blend of haegeum and electronic sounds has been praised by critics, including Beats 4 Peeps. Kim expanded her practice into performance curation with works like the sound installation Particles, Sound, Rhythm (2022), focusing on the materiality of instruments (Pal-eum), and the sensory-immersive performance Refill&amp;Refeel (2024–present). Her international presence includes performances at London’s Barbican Centre and a residency at Art Omi (NY) in 2024, as well as projects in Taiwan, Japan, and Berlin. In 2025, she will participate in the Festival Terres Vibrantes (France), a Soundscape research trip to Montreal (supported by ARKO), and a VCCA residency (USA). Recently, she presented Ear duct-us, a new work based on R. Murray Schafer’s soundscape theory.</p>
