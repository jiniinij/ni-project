Introduction: <p>Composer Taehyun Ha is wary of the superficial effects and arbitrary arrangements of color that are common pitfalls in contemporary music; instead, he seeks auditory inevitability. He refuses to let sound simply 'be,' striving to prove through musical form why a particular sound is required at a specific moment.</p><p>Instead of imitating the past, he reinterprets the tight logic and connectivity of traditional music into a modern syntax. His work fuses the dense developmental techniques of the 18th and 19th-century German tradition with a wide array of modern acoustic materials. This approach converts the abstruseness of modern music into clear, perceptible structures. Ultimately, it encourages active listening—sensing the direction of the sound—rather than passively consuming timbres or settling for the comforts of past emotions</p>
