Description: <p>For a contemporary music composer, 'intuition' is often dismissed as a dangerous term. In a landscape abounding with dissonance and noise, composers have long needed to arm themselves with the shield of rigorous logic and structural justification to persuade their listeners. I, too, have spent a long time constructing time while guarding against 'unjustified notes.' However, with Composed Impulse II, I sought to break away from established conventions and uncover raw, unvarnished sensations.</p><p>Paradoxically, this bold attempt was made possible by the project's 'strict limitations.' The 11 instrument samples provided to the six composers serve as an archive of contemporary techniques and stand as 'fixed materials' that cannot be altered. Due to the inherent nature of this archiving process, certain techniques exist only at specific pitches; this 'deficiency of material' thus became the very 'rule of composition.'</p><p>With limited sonic resources, I chose to follow the path led by the sounds themselves, rather than expending energy on constructing artificial structures. For the inherent textures and limited pitches of the materials were already paving the way. The pre-determined samples guided me, and I reacted to their sounds moment by moment. There was no need for me to intervene or twist the structure. I merely observed how these restricted materials clashed and mingled, selecting the most natural flows.</p><p>In this context, the work follows the form of a concerto, where a pre-constructed ensemble meets real-time performance. It does not merely replicate the traditional definition of a concerto where soloist and orchestra converse or compete. Instead, the 11 unalterable sampled instruments act as the orchestra, presenting a framework of restricted sounds and rhythms, over which the oboe performs the role of the soloist through improvisation.</p><p>Here, the microphone serves beyond simple amplification; it is an essential device to balance the breath and minute friction sounds of the reedless oboe against the rough textures of the ensemble. Ultimately, this piece is a record of the process showing how the intuition of both composer and performer reacts to and resonates with the physicality of sound within a restricted environment.</p>
